# E-commerce Funnel Analysis — Insights & Recommendations

## TL;DR
- Analyzed [4.2M] user events across [October 2019] from a cosmetics e-commerce store
- View → Cart drop-off ([34]%) is 3x bigger than Cart → Purchase drop-off
- Weekly retention decays to ~[6]% by week 4
- Browse-to-buy ratios vary 5x across categories — different UX problems for different categories

## Key Insight #1: The PDP, not Checkout, is the Real Leak
Most product teams instinctively optimize the checkout flow when conversion is low.
This data shows the opposite — [88]% of users drop between View and Cart, while only [22]% drop between Cart and Purchase.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize PDP A/B tests (reviews, social proof, sticky CTA) over checkout optimization for the next sprint.

## Key Insight #2: Cart Abandonment Has Two Modes
Of users who add to cart but don't purchase, ~30% actively remove items vs 70% who passively abandon.
These are two different psychological states requiring different interventions.

**Recommendation:**
- For active removers: Show total cost (incl. shipping) earlier in the flow
- For passive abandoners: Trigger reminder email at hour 4 + day 1

## Key Insight #3: Re-engagement Window is Days 5-7
Retention drops sharply between week 1 and week 2. Days 5-7 represent the last reachable window.

**Recommendation:** Trigger personalized re-engagement campaigns timed to days 5-7 of inactivity, with content tied to previously viewed categories.

## Key Insight #4: One Funnel Doesn't Fit All
High-consideration categories (premium skincare) browse 80:1 before buying. Impulse categories convert at 15:1.

**Recommendation:** Differentiate UX by category. Add review widgets and comparison tools for high-consideration; one-click checkout for impulse.

## Methodology
- 4M+ events from cosmetics e-commerce store (October 2019)
- DuckDB + SQL for analysis
- pandas + matplotlib for visualizations
- Cohort analysis: weekly cohorts, 4-week retention horizon
